Tết marks the beginning of the Lunar New Year and spring in northern Vietnam.

It started long ago with Vietnamese farmers in the Red River Delta, marking the start of a new season for growing rice.

Now, Tết is about new beginnings, family gatherings, and celebrating Vietnamese traditions.
The áo dài is a traditional Vietnamese garment known for its elegance and cultural significance. It is worn by all ages and genders, however, the design is dependent and may vary.

It consists of a long tunic worn over pants, generally reaching the lower leg area. The tunic has high slits on both sides that start from the waist.

The áo dài is often made from silk, cotton, or other fabrics. It can be worn for special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and ceremonies but can also be worn for everyday wear (typically cotton). It is also embroidered with beads, patterns and motifs.

Its design is a symbol of both beauty and tradition in Vietnamese culture.
